What are Blockchain technology & Zero-Knowledge Proofs?

Blockchain technology uses a digital ledger to store transactions that is secured & validated through the use of cryptography. The addition of Zero-Knowledge proofs adds a layer of security, validating information without the exchange of sensitive information

  • Verifiability

    All transactions conducted on the blockchain is available for all parties to view and verify that their vote has been casted, counted and tabulated in the final tally.

  • Immutable

    Transactions stored in the ledger of the blockchain cannot be altered easily. Once transactions are committed and pushed on chain, it will not be changed unless all nodes approve of the change

  • Risk Mitigation

    No exchange of personal data is necessary to prove the voter's identity, mitigating the risk associated with mishandling of personal data.
